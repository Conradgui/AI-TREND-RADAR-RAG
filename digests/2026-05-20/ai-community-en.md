**The central tension today is "vibe coding vs. production engineering."** Dev.to is buzzing with excitement (and some anxiety) about Google I/O '26—specifically Antigravity 2.0 and Gemini 3.5—but there is a strong undercurrent of concern about code quality and security. The "how do you prevent AI spaghetti code?" article is a clear signal that developers are loving the speed of AI-generated code but are terrified of the technical debt.

**On Lobste.rs, the sentiment is more skeptical and systems-focused.** The top posts are about *not* using AI for categorization, using safe languages like F# and OCaml, and revisiting deterministic systems (Incremental). This suggests that while the broader community is investing heavily in agents, the more experienced systems programmers are actively looking for ways to build guardrails and reliable fallbacks.

**Practical concerns are shifting.** It’s no longer about "should I use AI?" but rather "how do I build AI agents without breaking my OAuth flow?" and "how hard is it to deploy an LLM behind a Docker container?" The community is moving from experimentation towards operationalization, with a growing demand for tutorials on MLOps, policy files for agents, and per-user authorization.
